Author: John Doe

Institutiion:Green Earth University

Abstract

This paper explores the effects of climate change on agricultural practices, focusing on crop yields, soil health, and water resources. It highlights the challenges faced by farmers and suggests sustainable practices to mitigate these impacts.

Introduction

Climate change is a pressing global issue that significantly affects agriculture. Rising temperatures, changing precipitation patterns, and increased frequency of extreme weather events pose serious threats to food security and agricultural sustainability.

Methodology

This study employs a mixed-methods approach, combining quantitative data analysis of crop yields over the past two decades with qualitative interviews from farmers across different regions.

Results

The analysis reveals a downward trend in crop yields in regions most affected by climate change. Soil degradation and water scarcity are identified as major contributing factors.
